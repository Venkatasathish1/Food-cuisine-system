Ingredients:

  • 500 grams chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 cups basmati rice, washed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 cup mint leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 cup coriander leaves, chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 cups water

Procedure:

  1. Heat oil in a large pan and add cumin seeds.
  2. Add thinly sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and slit green chilies. Sauté for a minute.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft.
  5. Add coriander powder, turmeric powder, and garam masala. Mix well.
  6. Add chicken pieces and yogurt. Cook until chicken is partially cooked.
  7. Meanwhile, boil water in a separate pot and add soaked rice. Cook rice until it's 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  8. Once chicken is partially cooked, add chopped mint leaves and coriander leaves. Mix well.
  9. In a large pot, layer half of the cooked rice, followed by the cooked chicken mixture. Repeat the layers.
  10. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes, or until the chicken is fully cooked and the rice is tender.
  11. Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork and serve hot with raita or salan.
